## üìñ Reading Section

### Question Types (10 questions per passage)

#### 1. **Basic Information and Inferencing Questions**
- **Vocabulary Questions**: Test understanding of words in context
- **Reference Questions**: Identify what pronouns refer to
- **Factual Information**: Find explicitly stated information
- **Negative Factual Information**: Identify what is NOT mentioned
- **Inference Questions**: Understand implied information
- **Rhetorical Purpose**: Why the author mentions something
- **Sentence Simplification**: Choose equivalent meaning

#### 2. **Reading to Learn Questions** (Worth 2-3 points)
- **Insert Text Question**: Where does a sentence best fit?
- **Prose Summary**: Select 3 statements that best summarize (2 points)
- **Fill in a Table**: Organize information into categories (2-3 points)

### Reading Passage Characteristics
- **Length**: Approximately 700 words
- **Academic Topics**: Sciences, social sciences, arts, business
- **Organization Types**: 
  - Classification
  - Compare/contrast
  - Cause/effect
  - Problem/solution

### Reading Skills to Test
1. **Reading to Find Information**
   - Skimming for key facts
   - Scanning for specific details
   - Understanding vocabulary in context

2. **Basic Comprehension**
   - Main idea and major points
   - Important facts and details
   - Pronoun references

3. **Reading to Learn**
   - Understanding relationships between ideas
   - Recognizing organization and purpose
   - Inferring connections throughout the passage

### AI Generation Guidelines for Reading
```
- Generate 700-word passages on academic topics
- Include clear organization (cause/effect, compare/contrast, etc.)
- Create questions testing:
  * Vocabulary in context (define highlighted words)
  * Factual information (explicitly stated)
  * Inference (implied but not stated)
  * Purpose (why author mentions X)
  * Summary (main ideas)
- Provide detailed Japanese explanations
- Reference specific paragraphs in explanations
```

## üéß Listening Section

### Listening Materials

#### 1. **Academic Lectures** (4-5 minutes, 500-750 words)
- Professor does most talking, occasional student comments
- Or professor engages students in discussion
- Topics from various academic disciplines

#### 2. **Conversations** (3 minutes, 12-25 exchanges)
- **Office Hours**: Student with professor/TA about academic matters
- **Service Encounters**: Housing, registration, library
- **Student-to-Student**: Class projects, campus events

### Question Types (6 per lecture, 5 per conversation)

#### 1. **Basic Comprehension**
- Main purpose/topic
- Main ideas and supporting details
- Speaker's attitude/opinion

#### 2. **Pragmatic Understanding**
- Speaker's purpose (why does speaker say X?)
- Speaker's attitude
- Degree of certainty
- Function of statement

#### 3. **Connecting Information**
- Relationships between ideas (cause/effect, compare/contrast)
- Organization of information
- Inferences and conclusions
- Making connections
- Recognizing topic changes and digressions

### Listening Skills to Test
- Comprehend main idea and major points
- Understand important details
- Recognize speaker's attitude and certainty
- Understand function/purpose of statements
- Recognize organization
- Make inferences
- Recognize topic changes

### AI Generation Guidelines for Listening
```
- Create conversations with natural speech patterns
- Include:
  * Lectures: 500-750 words, 4-5 minutes
  * Conversations: 12-25 exchanges, 3 minutes
- Test comprehension of:
  * Main purpose and main ideas
  * Important details
  * Speaker's attitude/opinion
  * Why speaker says something (pragmatic)
  * Relationships between ideas
- Use Japanese for questions and explanations
- Include note-taking tips
```

## üé§ Speaking Section (17 minutes, 4 tasks)

### Speaking Task Types

#### **Task 1: Independent Speaking** (Paired Choice)
- **Preparation**: 15 seconds
- **Response**: 45 seconds
- Make and defend a personal choice between two options
- Based on personal experience and opinion

#### **Task 2: Integrated - Campus Situation** (Fit and Explain)
- **Reading**: Campus announcement/notice (80-110 words)
- **Listening**: Student conversation about it (60-80 seconds)
- **Preparation**: 30 seconds
- **Response**: 60 seconds
- Summarize speaker's opinion in context of reading

#### **Task 3: Integrated - Academic Course** (General/Specific)
- **Reading**: Academic term/concept definition (80-110 words)
- **Listening**: Lecture examples (60-90 seconds)
- **Preparation**: 30 seconds
- **Response**: 60 seconds
- Combine and convey information from both sources

#### **Task 4: Integrated - Academic Lecture** (Summary)
- **Listening**: Lecture excerpt (90-120 seconds)
- **Preparation**: 20 seconds
- **Response**: 60 seconds
- Summarize lecture and explain relationship between examples and topic

### Speaking Scoring Rubric (0-4 scale)

#### **Evaluation Criteria:**

1. **Delivery** (Clarity of speech)
   - Fluid and clear speech
   - Good pronunciation
   - Natural pacing
   - Natural intonation patterns

2. **Language Use** (Grammar and vocabulary)
   - Control of basic and complex structures
   - Appropriate vocabulary
   - Grammatical accuracy

3. **Topic Development** (Completeness and coherence)
   - Fully answer the question
   - Coherent presentation of ideas
   - Effective synthesis/summary (integrated tasks)
   - Clear progression of ideas

## ‚úçÔ∏è Writing Section (50 minutes, 2 tasks)

### Writing Task Types

#### **Task 1: Integrated Writing** (20 minutes)
- **Reading**: Academic passage (250-300 words, 3 minutes)
- **Listening**: Lecture on same topic, different perspective (250-320 words, 2 minutes)
- **Task**: Summarize listening passage and explain how it relates to reading
- **Expected Length**: 150-225 words (no penalty for more)

**Scoring Criteria:**
- Quality of writing (organization, grammar, vocabulary)
- Completeness and accuracy of content
- How well listening points relate to reading points

#### **Task 2: Independent Writing** (30 minutes)
- **Task**: State, explain, and support opinion on an issue
- **Expected Length**: Minimum 300 words
- **Common Question Types**:
  - "Do you agree or disagree...?"
  - "Some believe X, others believe Y. Which do you prefer?"

**Scoring Criteria:**
- Overall quality of writing
- Development of ideas
- Organization
- Grammar and vocabulary use
- Supporting reasons, examples, details

### Writing Scoring Rubric (0-5 scale)

#### **Level 5 (High)**
- Effectively addresses topic and task
- Well organized and well developed
- Appropriate reasons, examples, details
- Unity, progression, coherence
- Consistent facility in language use
- Minor lexical or grammatical errors

#### **Level 4 (Good)**
- Generally addresses topic/task
- Generally well organized and developed
- Appropriate reasons/examples (may lack detail)
- Generally displays unity and progression
- Occasional noticeable minor language errors

#### **Level 3 (Fair)**
- Addresses topic/task with some development
- Display of organization or connection of ideas
- Inappropriate details or insufficient detail
- Noticeable minor errors or some major errors

#### **Level 2 (Limited)**
- Limited development of topic
- Inadequate organization/connection
- Inappropriate or insufficient details
- Accumulation of errors in grammar/usage

#### **Level 1 (Weak)**
- Seriously limited in development
- Little organization
- Serious and frequent errors
- Difficult to understand

### AI Grading Guidelines for Writing
```
Independent Writing:
- Evaluate on: Development, Organization, Language Use
- Check for:
  * Clear thesis statement
  * Topic sentences for each paragraph
  * Supporting examples and details
  * Transitions between ideas
  * Varied sentence structures
  * Appropriate vocabulary
  * Grammatical accuracy

Integrated Writing:
- Evaluate on: Accuracy, Completeness, Language
- Check for:
  * Accurate summary of listening passage
  * Clear explanation of relationship to reading
  * No personal opinion (objective summary only)
  * Paraphrasing (not copying directly)
  * Coherent organization

Provide Japanese feedback with:
- Specific grammar corrections
- Vocabulary suggestions
- Organization improvements
- Content development tips
- Example sentences for improvement
```

## üìù General Test-Taking Strategies

### Time Management
- **Reading**: 54-72 minutes (3-4 passages)
- **Listening**: 41-57 minutes
- **Speaking**: 17 minutes
- **Writing**: 50 minutes

### Note-Taking
- Allowed and encouraged for all sections
- Use notes for answering questions
- Notes collected and destroyed after test

### Academic Skills Emphasized
1. **Integration of Skills**: Read + Listen + Speak/Write
2. **Academic Vocabulary**: Field-specific terminology
3. **Critical Thinking**: Analyze, synthesize, evaluate
4. **Organization**: Logical structure and coherence

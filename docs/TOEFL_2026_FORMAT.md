# TOEFL iBT 2026 New Format Guide

## Overview
TOEFL iBT transitioned to a shorter, more streamlined format in July 2023, with continuous updates leading to 2026.

## Test Duration
- **Total Time**: Approximately 2 hours (down from 3 hours)
- **Sections**: 4 (Reading, Listening, Speaking, Writing)

---

## Reading Section (35 minutes)
### Structure:
- **2 passages** (approximately 700 words each)
- **10 questions per passage** (20 total)
- **Time**: 35 minutes for both passages

### Question Types:
1. **Factual Information** (内容一致問題)
2. **Negative Factual Information** (内容不一致問題)
3. **Inference** (推論問題)
4. **Rhetorical Purpose** (意図問題)
5. **Vocabulary** (語彙問題)
6. **Reference** (指示語問題)
7. **Sentence Simplification** (言い換え問題)
8. **Insert Text** (挿入問題) - Uses [■] markers
9. **Prose Summary** (要約問題) - Select 3 correct answers from 6 options

### Scoring:
- **Raw Score**: 0-20 correct
- **Scaled Score**: 0-30 points
- **Time per Question**: ~1 minute 45 seconds

### Tips:
- Skim the passage first for main ideas
- Focus on topic sentences in each paragraph
- Eliminate obviously wrong answers
- Watch for extreme words (always, never, only)
- Connect keywords between question and passage

---

## Listening Section (36 minutes)
### Structure:
- **3 conversations** (2-3 minutes each, 5 questions each)
- **3 lectures** (3-5 minutes each, 6 questions each)
- **Total**: 6 audio tracks, 28 questions

### Question Types:
1. **Main Idea** (主旨問題)
2. **Detail** (詳細問題)
3. **Function** (機能問題) - Why does the speaker say X?
4. **Attitude** (態度問題)
5. **Organization** (構成問題)
6. **Inference** (推論問題)

### Scoring:
- **Raw Score**: 0-28 correct
- **Scaled Score**: 0-30 points
- **Time per Question**: ~1 minute 17 seconds

### Tips:
- Take notes actively (organize by topic)
- Listen for signal words (however, for example, in contrast)
- Focus on main ideas, not minor details
- Pay attention to tone and emphasis
- Watch for comparisons and cause-effect relationships

---

## Speaking Section (16 minutes)
### Structure:
- **Task 1**: Independent Speaking (15 sec prep, 45 sec response)
  - Types: Agree/Disagree, Preference, Hypothetical, Opinion, Describe
- **Task 2**: Integrated (Reading + Listening, 30 sec prep, 60 sec response)
  - Campus announcement + student conversation
- **Task 3**: Integrated (Reading + Listening, 30 sec prep, 60 sec response)
  - Academic concept + lecture example
- **Task 4**: Integrated (Listening only, 20 sec prep, 60 sec response)
  - Lecture summary

### Scoring Rubric (0-4 scale per task, converted to 0-30):
- **Delivery**: Clear pronunciation, natural pacing, intonation
- **Language Use**: Grammar accuracy, vocabulary variety
- **Topic Development**: Coherence, organization, supporting details

### Tips:
- Use templates for structure (e.g., "The reading states that... The lecture challenges this by...")
- Practice speaking for exactly 45/60 seconds
- Use transition words (First, Additionally, In conclusion)
- Avoid long pauses - keep speaking
- Focus on fluency over perfection

---

## Writing Section (29 minutes)
### Structure:
- **Task 1**: Integrated Writing (20 minutes)
  - Read passage (3 min) + Listen to lecture (2-3 min) + Write response (20 min)
  - 150-225 words recommended
  - Summarize how lecture challenges/supports reading

- **Task 2**: Academic Discussion (10 minutes) **[NEW FORMAT]**
  - Professor posts discussion question
  - 2 students post responses
  - You write your own response (100+ words)
  - Must engage with others' ideas and add your own perspective

### Scoring Rubric (0-5 scale per task, converted to 0-30):
- **Development**: Clear thesis, supporting details, examples
- **Organization**: Logical structure, transitions, coherence
- **Language Use**: Grammar, vocabulary, sentence variety

### Academic Discussion Tips:
- Address the professor's question directly
- Reference at least one classmate's idea
- Add your own unique perspective
- Use academic vocabulary
- Structure: Greeting → Acknowledge others → Your opinion → Supporting reason → Conclusion

### Integrated Writing Tips:
- Note-taking is crucial during reading and listening
- Use reporting verbs (states, argues, claims, contradicts)
- Focus on the relationship between reading and lecture
- Don't include personal opinion
- Template: "The reading passage discusses... However, the lecturer challenges this by..."

## Scoring Scale
### Individual Sections:
- **Reading**: 0-30
- **Listening**: 0-30
- **Speaking**: 0-30
- **Writing**: 0-30

### Total Score: 0-120
- **Advanced (C1)**: 95+
- **Upper Intermediate (B2)**: 72-94
- **Intermediate (B1)**: 42-71
- **Basic (A2)**: Below 42

### Score Interpretation:
- **28-30**: Advanced - Near-native proficiency
- **24-27**: High Intermediate - Strong academic skills
- **18-23**: Intermediate - Adequate for basic communication
- **12-17**: Low Intermediate - Needs improvement
- **0-11**: Beginning - Significant development needed

## Test Day Tips
1. **Time Management**:
   - Reading: ~1:45 per question
   - Listening: Focus during audio, quick answers
   - Speaking: Use all prep time, speak full time
   - Writing: 20 min for Task 1, 10 min for Task 2

2. **Note-Taking**:
   - Listening: Organize by main idea and supporting details
   - Speaking Integrated: Brief keywords only
   - Writing Integrated: Detailed notes on 3 main points

3. **Strategic Approaches**:
   - Reading: Skip and return to difficult questions
   - Listening: Predict content based on introduction
   - Speaking: Record yourself practicing beforehand
   - Writing: Leave 2-3 minutes for proofreading

4. **Common Mistakes to Avoid**:
   - Spending too much time on one question
   - Not using all available time for responses
   - Copying exact phrases from reading passages
   - Including personal opinion in Integrated Writing
   - Speaking too quietly or too fast

---

## Practice Strategies
1. **Daily Practice**:
   - Read academic articles (15-20 min)
   - Listen to lectures/podcasts with note-taking
   - Record speaking responses and self-evaluate
   - Write timed responses to sample prompts

2. **Weekly Goals**:
   - Complete 1-2 full practice tests
   - Review all incorrect answers
   - Track performance by question type
   - Build vocabulary lists (academic words)

3. **Resources**:
   - ETS Official TOEFL Practice Materials
   - Academic journals and university lectures
   - TED Talks and educational YouTube channels
   - TOEFL prep books (Kaplan, Barron's, TST Prep)

## Updated Content Guidelines for AI Generation
### Reading Passages:
- Length: 650-750 words
- Tone: Academic, objective
- Topics: Science, History, Arts, Social Sciences
- Include clear thesis and supporting paragraphs
- Use advanced vocabulary (C1-C2 level)
- Insert [■] markers for Insert Text questions

### Listening Transcripts:
- Conversations: Natural dialogue with filler words
- Lectures: Organized with clear structure and examples
- Use signposting language (First, Next, In conclusion)
- Include rhetorical questions and emphasis
- Provide Japanese translation for practice mode

### Speaking Prompts:
- Independent: Relatable topics for students
- Integrated: Clear reading + coherent lecture
- Provide sample high-scoring responses as models

### Writing Tasks:
- Integrated: 3 clear contrasting points
- Academic Discussion: Realistic professor question + 2 diverse student responses
- Sample responses demonstrate organization and language use
